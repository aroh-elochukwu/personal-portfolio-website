About Me

My names are Aroh Innocent Elochukwu, I am the youngest of Six male children in a Catholic, loving, Nigerian Home. I am a computer science graduate of Benson Idahosa University, who upon graduation went to work with the Delta State government as a Data Analyst for its Health Insurance Scheme. A while ago, I identified the need to further develop my IT skills, to enable my professional growth. Whilst plotting my path my elder brother recommended the Manitoba Institute of Trades and Technology (MITT) as the next step to reaching these set goals. Fast-forward many months later, I am on my laptop writing my first assignment for SD 160 (Technical writing Skills) for a Software developer degree at MITT.

Growing up in Southern Nigeria (city of Warri to be precise), IT has always appealed to me because of the convenience and efficiency it introduced into our lives. Which has spurred every section of life to improve upon its prior established ideology, philosophies, and culture to yield positive/better outcomes and results. Who would have thought that we could sit in front of our computers and host a crystal-clear video conference call with crisp audio clarity between people at different ends of the World?

I categorize myself as an ambivert whose behavior is mostly dependent on where or with whom I am in their company. I am passionate about every beautiful thing life has to offer and my hobbies include table tennis, soccer, and meeting/interacting with exciting personalities.
